Acceuil Histoire de Taïwan Lien 3 Noah Lefevre

6,000 years ago, the first populations were Austronesian peoples from eastern China who brought Austronesian languages to the island. Over the past 400 years, the native Taiwanese have been the object of much discrimination at the hands of Westerners and the Chinese.

The first Western contact with Taiwan took place in 1452. A Portuguese expedition en route to Japan sailed off the island. Captivated by its allure, the sailors on board are said to have said << Ilha formosa >> on sighting it, Either << the beautiful island >>.
